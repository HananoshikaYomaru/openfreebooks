Circle theorems connect angles, chords, tangents, and arcs. They are essential for geometry proofs and exam reasoning.

Key circle theorems

  • Angle at center is twice angle at circumference on same arc.
  • Angles in the same segment are equal.
  • Angle in a semicircle is \(90^\circ\).
  • Opposite angles of cyclic quadrilateral sum to \(180^\circ\).
  • Tangent is perpendicular to radius at point of contact.
  • Two tangents from same external point are equal in length.
  • Alternate segment theorem: angle between tangent and chord equals angle in opposite arc.

Using theorems in proofs

  1. Mark known equal angles/lengths clearly.
  2. Name theorem used at each step.
  3. Chain results to target statement.

Cyclic quadrilaterals

A quadrilateral is cyclic if all vertices lie on one circle.

Useful tests:

  • Opposite angles supplementary.
  • Exterior angle equals opposite interior angle.

Tangent-chord applications

When a tangent touches a chord endpoint, use alternate segment theorem to connect tangent angle with an inscribed angle subtending that chord.

Exam strategy

  • Label all equal angles immediately.
  • Write theorem names, not just numbers.
  • Avoid assuming symmetry unless given or proved.
  • Finish with a clear conclusion sentence.
