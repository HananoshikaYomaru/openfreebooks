History

Circle theorems were developed in classical geometry and refined through Euclidean proofs. They remain central in modern school geometry due to their structured reasoning style.

Checkpoints

If central angle is \(100^\circ\), what is inscribed angle on same arc?

Answer: \(50^\circ\).

In a cyclic quadrilateral, one angle is \(72^\circ\). Find opposite angle.

Answer: \(108^\circ\).

What angle is formed by radius and tangent at point of contact?

Answer: \(90^\circ\).

Two tangents from external point \(P\) touch at \(A,B\). Compare \(PA\) and \(PB\).

Answer: \(PA=PB\).

Why does one theorem name per step improve proof quality?

Answer: It makes logical justification explicit and verifiable.

Applications

  • Design: arcs and tangency in mechanical/architectural drawings.
  • Optics: circular geometry in lenses and reflective paths.
  • CAD tools: tangent/chord constraints in modeling software.
